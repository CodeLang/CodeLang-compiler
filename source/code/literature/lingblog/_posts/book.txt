It begins with a copy.
A duplicated thought.
An idea replicated.
An ability to re-conceptualize a constituent previously conceptualized.
To have the same thought moment twice.
A replication that is quickly followed by collapse.
A merging operation.
The ability to merge a constituent to itself resulting in a newly merged constituent.
A single sign combined to itself to create a new sign.
To count is to participate in the act of copying and merging signs in repetition.
Count = Copy -> Merge
A performance which seems intrinsically dependent on the continuum of time.
